Privacy Policy

Apps Privacy

Last updated: July 10, 2026

Your privacy matters. Marble is a local-first EPUB reader. It is designed to work without an account, without tracking, and without sending your data anywhere.

Short Version

  • We do not collect any personal data.
  • We do not use analytics SDKs or third-party trackers.
  • We do not require an account or sign-up.
  • Your books, reading data, and annotations stay on your device unless you choose to sync.
  • Optional iCloud sync uses Apple's CloudKit with end-to-end encryption.

Information We Collect

None. Marble does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information on any server we operate. No analytics SDKs, no crash reporting services (beyond Apple's optional built-in system), no advertising identifiers, no tracking.

Data Stored on Your Device

Marble stores data locally in its sandboxed container:

  • Book files you import (EPUB, PDF, MOBI, AZW3, CBR, CBZ, DOCX, TXT)
  • Reading progress and library metadata (collections, tags, ratings, reading status)
  • Annotations (highlights, margin notes) and reading statistics
  • App settings including per-book typography preferences (font, size, spacing, themes)
  • Cloud storage authentication tokens (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, WebDAV) — stored in iOS Keychain
  • Calibre server connection details and OPDS catalog URLs

All this data stays on your device. We cannot access it.

iCloud Sync

Marble offers optional iCloud sync for reading position, library metadata, and annotations across your iPhone and iPad. This uses Apple's CloudKit framework with end-to-end encryption. Your data is stored in your personal iCloud account under your control. You can disable iCloud sync at any time in Settings. No sync data is accessible to us.

Third-Party Services

Marble can connect to services you explicitly configure:

  • Cloud Storage — Import books from Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, or WebDAV. Authentication uses OAuth 2.0 tokens stored in the iOS Keychain. We do not have access to your cloud accounts.
  • OPDS Catalogs — Browse public and private OPDS catalogs. We do not operate any catalog servers.
  • Calibre Content Server — Wirelessly sync with your own Calibre server over your local network.

Crash Reporting

We do not use third-party crash reporting. If you opt in to share crash reports with Apple via the standard iOS dialog, that data goes to Apple under their privacy policy. We do not receive it unless you send it to us.

In-App Purchases

Purchases are processed by Apple via the App Store. We do not receive or store payment information. Receipt data is verified locally on your device to unlock features — it is not sent to any server we control.
